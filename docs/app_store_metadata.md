## Long Description
Don't Let Them In is a top-down roguelike tower defense game about surviving one very bad night in a very weird house. Aliens are pouring in through doors, windows, vents, and any weak spot they can find. Your job is simple: protect the Safe Room at all costs.

Every run plays across three connected floors with distinct layouts and pressure curves. The Ground Floor gives you room to stabilize, the Upper Floor forces tighter lane control, and the Attic becomes a final stand where every placement decision matters. Between waves, you spend Scrap to build defenses, patch weak points, and prepare for the next push. Between floors, you draft upgrades and perks that shape the rest of the run.

Your loadout spans four defense styles so no single strategy solves everything. Improvised traps deliver big one-time burst damage and can control choke points. Mounted weapons provide reliable sustained fire and reward smart coverage. Pets are mobile defenders that pressure flanks and disrupt advancing enemies. Smart home tech adds utility, control, and chaos, but overloading tech can trigger power surge penalties. As waves escalate, special alien behaviors force adaptation: stealth stalkers, hacking tech units, and breach-capable attackers can break comfortable plans quickly.

Progress carries beyond a single run. End-of-run performance awards Salvage Points used in Meta Upgrades that permanently expand your options: stronger starts, better traps, improved draft picks, and more. Campaign difficulty tiers and endless progression add long-term goals for players who want to optimize and push deeper.

The tone stays tense but playful. The world is dangerous, but the solutions are absurd in the best way: paint-can traps, improvised mounts, loyal pets, and defensive tech that is sometimes almost as hazardous as the aliens. If you like tactical planning, run-based progression, and dark comedy, Don't Let Them In is built for you.

### Key Features
- Three-floor roguelike campaign structure with escalating layouts and enemy pressure
- Four defense categories: traps, weapons, pets, and smart home tech
- Draft picks between floors for run-defining upgrades and perks
- Multiple alien classes with special mechanics (stealth, hacking, breaching, boss waves)
- Meta progression with persistent Salvage Points and permanent unlocks
- Endless mode and difficulty tiers for replayability and mastery
